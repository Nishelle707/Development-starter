Holy Family Convent (HFC) in Bambalapitiya was founded in 1903 by the Sisters of the Holy Family, who were invited to establish a girls' English school. It started in a rented house with one classroom and has since grown to include a boarding school, science lab, chapel, and various other facilities. The school has a long history of education and extracurricular activities, and its past pupils' association was established in 1934

  • Origins: It began in a rented house on Laurie's Road with a single classroom and 28 students, both girls and boys.
  • Expansion: The school has expanded significantly over time and now includes a boarding school, kindergarten, science lab, chapel, art room, library, assembly hall, and sports facilities.
  • Past Pupils Association (PPA): The PPA was inaugurated on March 17, 1934, and is active in supporting the school through various fundraisers.
  • School culture: The school's vision is to "light up the world with women of virtue and wisdom" and it aims to develop creative, entrepreneurial, and balanced individuals.
  • Going private: The school opted to "go private" in 1960 under the Schools Take Over Act, which required it to find ways to raise funds to continue its work.
